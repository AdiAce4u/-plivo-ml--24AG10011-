# Notes

The fitness function is a composite of Resemblyzer speaker similarity, and anti-gaming penalties for raspiness (spectral flatness/HF ratio/jitter), off-manifold drift (distance to PCA subspace), duration mismatch, and cross-sentence instability. Similarity scores plateaued primarily because of the manifold constraint; off-manifold tensors artificially spike raw Resemblyzer similarity while sounding highly degraded and raspy to humans. By strictly projecting candidates back into the 95% variance PCA subspace of the 54 stock voices, and penalizing spectral flatness, the search safely explores realistic voice variations without collapsing into high-frequency buzz. Furthermore, leveraging `dim_groups.json` allowed the simulated annealing to take larger steps on dimensions with high 'timbre' leverage while freezing 'prosody' dimensions, leading to a much more sample-efficient search within our limited 150-evaluation budget.

## Listening Notes
- **Adversarial Raspiness:** Without spectral flatness penalties, the optimizer frequently discovers tensors that score >0.95 on Resemblyzer similarity but sound like harsh, metallic robotic buzzing. The encoder is completely blind to this audio degradation, highlighting a severe misalignment between embedding distance and perceptual quality.
- **Prosodic Speed-Glitching:** Modifying dimensions in the 128-255 range too aggressively causes the iSTFT Decoder to swallow vowels and speak at 3x speed. The audio becomes unintelligible, but occasionally passes ASR if the words are short enough.
- **Off-Manifold Breathiness / Mumbling:** Moving too far outside the PCA bounds of the stock voices causes severe breathiness and mumbling, where the speaker sounds out of breath or is whispering. This is prevented by enforcing the PCA manifold constraint.
